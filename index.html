Who I am

About Me

I'm Pranav Sachin Sonawane, a first-year B.Tech student specialising in Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning at Sanjivani University, Kopargaon, Maharashtra, India.

My journey started with a simple question: can machines truly learn like humans? That curiosity pushed me to explore Python, dive into neural networks, solder my first ESP32, and compete in national hackathons — all within my first year of college.

I'm deeply passionate about the intersection of AI, Robotics, IoT, and Process Automation. Whether that's training a classification model or wiring up a robot that climbs vertical walls, I love turning ambitious ideas into physical reality.

Outside tech, I explore how automation solves real-world challenges — from precision agriculture to accessibility tools. I'm actively looking for internships, open-source contributions, and collaborative projects that push my limits.

Wall Climbing Robot

An IoT-based autonomous robot capable of climbing vertical surfaces using a pneumatic suction mechanism. Combines real-time sensor feedback with precision motor control for wall navigation. Designed, built, and programmed entirely from scratch as a proof-of-concept for adhesion robotics.

Maze Solver Game (Multiplayer)

A Python-based multiplayer maze-solving game where players compete to find the shortest path through procedurally generated mazes. Features real-time networking, custom maze-generation algorithms (DFS/BFS), and a polished visual interface — built to sharpen algorithm design skills.
